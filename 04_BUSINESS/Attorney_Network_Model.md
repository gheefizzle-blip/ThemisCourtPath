## Critical Finding: Arizona Is Uniquely Favorable

**As of January 1, 2021, Arizona eliminated Rule 7.2(b) and Rule 5.4**, allowing:
- Attorneys to pay referral fees to non-lawyers
- Non-lawyer ownership of law firms (Alternative Business Structure — ABS program)

This makes Arizona one of the most favorable states in the US for Themis's attorney referral model. What would be illegal in most states is explicitly permitted in Arizona.

## Themis Revenue Model

### Consultation Booking
- User completes filing → prompted: "Want a licensed attorney to review your documents?"
- **User pays**: $49-99 for 20-minute Zoom consultation with matched AZ family law attorney
- **Themis keeps**: $15-25 per consultation (booking/platform fee)
- **Attorney receives**: $34-74 per consultation

### Conversion Fee
- If user hires attorney for full representation after consultation:
- **Themis receives**: Flat $500 conversion fee
- **Alternative**: 12% of first-year billings (higher but harder to track)
- **Recommended**: Flat $500 — simpler, more predictable, easier to enforce

### Compliance Requirements (Arizona)
- Fees must be reasonable (not unconscionable)
- Client must consent in writing to referral fee arrangement
- At least one Arizona-licensed attorney must be involved
- Fee arrangement must be disclosed to client
