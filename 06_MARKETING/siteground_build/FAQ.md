## 1. Is this legal advice?

No. Themis Court Path is a document preparation tool. We do not provide legal advice and we do not represent you in court. The forms we fill out are the same official Arizona court forms you could get from the county self-service center. We just make filling them out faster and less error-prone. For advice about your specific case, consult a licensed Arizona attorney.

## 2. Is my data secure?

Yes. Three layers of protection:

1. Your intake data is encrypted (Fernet / AES) before it is stored. The raw database row contains ciphertext, not your personal information.
2. Generated PDFs are produced in memory and streamed directly to you. Nothing is written to disk in plaintext.
3. We never see your card information. Stripe handles payment processing directly.

## 3. Can I edit my documents after they are generated?

Yes. We provide both an editable PDF and a court-ready filled PDF. Review every field before you take the documents to the court.

## 4. What is your refund policy?

Full refund available before your documents are generated. After generation, the service has been delivered, so refunds are not issued except in the case of a software error on our end. The complete policy is in our Terms of Service.

## 5. Which courts do you support?

Today: Navajo County, Arizona. Coming next: Maricopa, Pima, and Pinal counties (those three cover roughly 75 percent of Arizona's population). The state-standard form covers many more counties; we are rolling out support in priority order.

## 6. How do I actually file the documents?

Two options:

1. Print the documents and take them to the Clerk of Superior Court in your county. Pay the court's filing fee (typically $200 to $350).
2. File electronically through Arizona's eFileAZ system at efile.azcourts.gov.

We provide step-by-step instructions for both inside the app. Themis Court Path does not file documents on your behalf.

## 7. Do I need a lawyer?

For straightforward child support petitions, no. Many people file pro se (on their own) every year. If your case involves disputed paternity, complex custody, significant assets, or any element of domestic violence, we strongly recommend consulting a licensed Arizona attorney.

## 8. What if I make a mistake during the intake?

You can review and edit any field before generating documents. Each section has a Back button. You can also start a new filing at any time.

## 9. What happens if I do not finish my intake in one sitting?

You can save your progress, sign out, and come back later. Your data stays encrypted in storage until you complete the intake.

## 10. What about the other parent? Do they create an account too?

No. Only the petitioner (the person filing) creates an account. The respondent (the other parent) is named in the documents but does not need an account on Themis Court Path.
