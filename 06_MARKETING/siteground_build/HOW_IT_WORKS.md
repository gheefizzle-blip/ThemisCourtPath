## Step 1: Create your account

What happens:
You give us an email and a password. That is all we need to create your account. No credit card, no commitment.

Why this step exists:
Your filing data has to belong to someone. The account ties your filing to you so only you can access it later.

Action button (this step only):
Label: Create Account
URL:   https://app.themiscourtpath.com/auth/register

---

## Step 2: Complete the guided intake

What happens:
We ask you 11 short sections of questions. Names, dates of birth, employment, income, parenting time, and so on. Each question has a plain-language explanation. You can save your progress and come back later.

What you should have ready:
- Both parents' names, dates of birth, and addresses
- The child's full name and date of birth
- Both parents' employer information
- Monthly gross income for each parent (if known)
- A rough sense of the parenting time split

Time required:
About 25 minutes if you have your information ready. Longer if you need to look things up.

---

## Step 3: Pay the $99 filing fee

What happens:
After your intake is complete, you pay a flat $99 through Stripe. We never see your card information.

What this $99 covers:
Themis Court Path's document preparation work. It does not cover the court's separate filing fee, which you pay directly to the court when you file.

---

## Step 4: Download your court-ready documents

What happens:
After payment, you can download:
- An editable PDF of the petition packet
- A court-ready filled PDF
- A validation report listing every field that was filled

Documents are generated in memory and streamed directly to your browser. Nothing is stored as a plaintext file on our servers.

---

## Step 5: File the documents with the court

What happens:
You take the documents to the Clerk of Superior Court in your county and file them. You can also file electronically through Arizona's eFileAZ system. We tell you exactly which steps to follow for your county.

What we do not do:
Themis Court Path does not file documents on your behalf. We prepare the documents. You file them.

What about the court's filing fee?
The court charges a separate filing fee, typically $200 to $350 depending on the county. If you cannot afford it, Arizona has fee deferral and waiver programs. We provide instructions for applying.
