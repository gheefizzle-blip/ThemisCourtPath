---

You've filled out the paperwork. You've calculated the child support amount. Now you're standing at the courthouse with a stack of forms, wondering: what happens next?

Filing a Petition to Establish Child Support starts a legal process with several distinct steps. Here's what to expect at each stage.

---

## Stage 1: Filing (Day 1)

### What Happens
You bring your completed petition packet to the Clerk of Superior Court in your county (or file electronically through eFileAZ). The clerk:

- Reviews your forms for completeness (not accuracy — that's your responsibility)
- Assigns a **case number**
- Stamps your documents with the filing date
- Collects the filing fee
- Returns your copies (you keep one, one goes to the respondent)

### What to Bring
- Original petition plus 2 copies
- Photo ID
- Filing fee (check your county — typically $200-350)
- Fee waiver/deferral application if you can't afford the fee

### How Long
The filing itself takes 15-30 minutes at the clerk's window.

---

## Stage 2: Service of Process (Days 1-30)

### What Happens
After filing, you must officially notify the other parent by delivering copies of the petition. This is called "service of process."

### Who Can Serve
- A certified process server ($80-175)
- The county sheriff's office
- Any person over 18 who is NOT a party to the case
- **You cannot serve the papers yourself**

### Methods
- **Personal service**: Someone hands the documents directly to the respondent. Most reliable.
- **Certified mail**: Some courts allow service by certified mail with return receipt.
- **Service by publication**: If you truly cannot find the respondent after diligent efforts, you can publish notice in a newspaper. This is a last resort and requires court approval.

### After Service
The process server files a **Proof of Service** (also called "Affidavit of Service") with the court, confirming when and how the respondent was served.

### Timeline
The respondent has **20 days** (if served in Arizona) or **30 days** (if served outside Arizona) to file a response.

---

## Stage 3: Response Period (Days 20-30 After Service)

### If the Respondent Responds
The respondent may file a written response agreeing, disagreeing, or proposing different terms. If they disagree with the support amount, they'll need to provide their own financial information and worksheet.

### If the Respondent Doesn't Respond (Default)
If the respondent doesn't respond within the deadline, you can request a **default judgment**. The court may grant your petition as filed — meaning the support amount you calculated becomes the order. This is the most common outcome for uncontested cases.

---

## Stage 4: Hearing (4-8 Weeks After Filing)

### What Happens
The court schedules a hearing before a judge or commissioner. Both parents are expected to attend.

At the hearing, the judge:
- Reviews both parents' financial information
- Examines the Child Support Worksheet
- Asks questions about income, employment, and parenting time
- May consider deviations from the Guidelines amount
- Issues a child support order

### What to Bring
- Your copy of the filed petition
- Proof of income (pay stubs, tax returns, bank statements)
- Proof of expenses (childcare receipts, insurance premiums)
- Proof of Service
- Any other supporting documents

### How Long
Most child support hearings last 15-30 minutes for straightforward cases. Complex cases may take longer.

### What the Judge Looks At
- Are both incomes accurately reported?
- Is the worksheet calculation correct?
- Are there reasons to deviate from the Guidelines?
- Who provides health insurance?
- What is the parenting time arrangement?
- Are there extraordinary expenses (special needs, travel)?

---

## Stage 5: The Child Support Order

### What It Contains
The judge's order will specify:
- Monthly child support amount
- Who pays and who receives
- When payments begin (usually the first of the following month)
- Health insurance responsibilities
- How non-covered medical expenses are split
- How payments are made (through the Support Payment Clearinghouse)
- Tax exemption allocation

### Clearinghouse Fee
Arizona charges an $8/month fee for processing payments through the Support Payment Clearinghouse. This is added to the monthly support amount.

### Enforcement
If the paying parent doesn't pay, the receiving parent can:
- Contact the Division of Child Support Services (DCSS)
- Request wage garnishment through the employer
- File a contempt action with the court
- The state can suspend driver's licenses and professional licenses for non-payment

---

## Stage 6: After the Order

### Modifying the Order
Child support orders can be modified if there's a "substantial and continuing change in circumstances":
- Job loss or significant income change (±15%)
- Change in parenting time
- Child's needs change (medical, educational)
- Remarriage doesn't typically qualify on its own

### Duration
Child support in Arizona generally continues until the child:
- Turns 18 and graduates high school, OR
- Turns 19, whichever comes first
- A child with disabilities may receive support longer
