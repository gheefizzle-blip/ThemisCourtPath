Every year, over 50,000 child support cases are filed in Arizona. Many of those parents can't afford the $2,000-3,000 an attorney would charge to handle the paperwork. The good news? You don't need one.

Arizona courts are designed to be accessible to self-represented litigants (called "pro se" parties). The court system provides official forms, self-help centers, and step-by-step instructions. Here's your complete guide to filing for child support in Arizona without a lawyer.

## Step 1: Determine Which County to File In

In Arizona, you file your petition in the county where you or the other parent lives. This is called "jurisdiction." You need at least one of these to be true:

- The respondent (other parent) lives in the county
- The respondent can be served with papers in the county
- The child was conceived in the county
- The child lives in the county
- The respondent lived with the child in the county

If you're unsure, your home county is usually the right choice. The petition form has checkboxes for each jurisdictional basis.

## Step 2: Gather Your Information

Before you start filling out forms, collect:

- **Both parents' information**: Full legal names, dates of birth, Social Security numbers, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses
- **Children's information**: Full names, dates of birth, Social Security numbers
- **Employment details**: Both parents' employers, addresses, phone numbers
- **Income information**: Both parents' gross monthly income (before taxes)
- **Insurance**: Who provides health insurance for the children, monthly premium cost
- **Childcare costs**: Monthly daycare or childcare expenses
- **Parenting time**: How many overnights each parent has per year

## Step 3: Get the Official Forms

Arizona uses a standard petition packet that includes:

- **Sensitive Data Sheet** (confidential page with SSNs and DOBs)
- **Family Court Cover Sheet** (case classification)
- **Petition to Establish Child Support** (the main filing)
- **Parent's Worksheet for Child Support** (the calculation page)
- **Order to Appear** (notice to the other parent)
- **Child Support Order** (proposed order for the judge)
- **Employer Information Sheet** (for wage garnishment)

You can get these forms from your county's Self-Service Center, from azcourts.gov, or use Themis Court Path to fill them out automatically.

## Step 4: Calculate Child Support Using the Arizona Guidelines

This is the step that trips up most people. Arizona uses a formula based on:

1. Both parents' adjusted gross monthly income
2. Number of children
3. Parenting time (overnights per year)
4. Medical insurance costs
5. Childcare costs

The calculation involves looking up values in the Arizona Schedule based on combined income, applying a proportionate share formula, and making adjustments. It's 25+ line items of math.

## Step 5: Fill Out the Petition Packet

Work through each page of the petition, filling in your information. Pay careful attention to:

- **Checkboxes**: The petition has dozens of checkboxes for paternity method, jurisdiction, requests to the court, insurance, and payment terms. Miss one and the clerk may reject your filing.
- **The Worksheet**: This must be mathematically correct. The judge will use it to determine the support amount.
- **Consistency**: Your name, address, and case details must match across all pages.

## Step 6: File with the Clerk of Court

Take your completed forms to the Clerk of Superior Court in your county. You'll need:

- Original forms plus 2 copies (one for you, one for the respondent)
- Filing fee (varies by county, typically $200-350)
- Photo ID

**Can't afford the filing fee?** Arizona offers fee deferral and fee waiver programs. Ask the clerk for a Fee Deferral/Waiver application.

**Electronic filing**: Most Arizona counties accept e-filing through eFileAZ (efile.azcourts.gov). This lets you file from your computer without visiting the courthouse.

## Step 7: Serve the Respondent

After filing, you must deliver copies to the other parent. This is called "service of process." You CANNOT serve the papers yourself — it must be done by:

- A certified process server ($80-175)
- The county sheriff's office
- A person over 18 who is not a party to the case
- Certified mail with return receipt (in some cases)

After service, the process server files a "Proof of Service" with the court.

## Step 8: Attend the Hearing

The court will schedule a hearing (usually 4-8 weeks after filing). At the hearing:

- Both parents present their financial information
- The judge reviews the worksheet calculations
- The judge issues a child support order
- The order includes the monthly amount, insurance requirements, and payment terms
