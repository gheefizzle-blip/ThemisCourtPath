## Frequently Asked Questions

### General

**What is Themis Court Path?**
Themis Court Path is an online document preparation platform that helps you fill out official Arizona court forms for family law cases. You answer questions in plain English, and we automatically populate the correct court forms with your information — including all the math for the Child Support Worksheet.

**Is Themis Court Path a law firm?**
No. Themis Court Path is a document preparation tool, not a law firm. We do not provide legal advice, evaluate your case, or represent you in court. We help you fill out the same official forms you'd get from the court's self-help center — but faster and with fewer mistakes.

**Who is this for?**
Anyone who needs to file family court documents in Arizona: parents seeking child support, individuals going through divorce, or anyone who wants to prepare their own court paperwork without hiring an attorney. Paralegals and attorneys also use the platform to speed up document preparation.

**How long does it take?**
Most users complete the intake wizard in 20-30 minutes. Your completed documents are generated instantly after you finish.

### Legal Questions

**Is this legal advice?**
No. We provide document preparation only. The questions we ask come directly from the official court forms. The calculations use the published Arizona Child Support Guidelines. We do not advise you on what to enter — that's your responsibility. For legal advice, consult a licensed Arizona attorney.

**Do I need a lawyer?**
Not for straightforward child support petitions. Many people successfully file pro se (on their own) every year. However, if your case involves disputed paternity, complex custody arrangements, domestic violence, or significant assets, consulting an attorney is strongly recommended. We offer a $99 attorney consultation add-on.

**Can I get in trouble for filing my own paperwork?**
No. You have the legal right to represent yourself in court (pro se). Arizona courts have self-help centers specifically to assist self-represented litigants. You are responsible for the accuracy of information in your filing.

### About the Calculator

**How does the child support calculator work?**
The calculator uses the official Arizona Child Support Guidelines (2022 revision, effective January 2023). It looks up the basic support obligation based on both parents' combined adjusted gross income and number of children, then applies adjustments for medical insurance, childcare, and parenting time.

**Is the calculator amount what the judge will order?**
The Guidelines amount is the presumptive starting point. The judge may deviate from this amount based on specific factors in your case (special needs, extreme income disparity, etc.), but in most cases the Guidelines amount is what gets ordered.

**What's the difference between Table A and Table B?**
Table A applies when the non-custodial parent has fewer than 110 overnight visits per year. Table B applies when they have 110 or more overnights. Table B results in a lower support amount because the non-custodial parent has more parenting time and associated costs.

### Courts and Filing

**Which courts do you support?**
Currently: Navajo County, Arizona. We're expanding to all 15 Arizona counties — Maricopa, Pima, and Pinal are next. The core forms are similar statewide.

**How do I file my documents after generating them?**
Download your completed PDF, print it, and file it at your county's Clerk of Superior Court office. You'll need to pay a filing fee (varies by county, typically $200-350). We provide specific filing instructions for your county. Coming soon: electronic filing through eFileAZ.

**What about filing fees?**
Court filing fees are paid directly to the court — not to us. If you can't afford the filing fee, Arizona offers fee deferral and waiver programs. We'll provide instructions on how to apply.

**What happens after I file?**
After filing, the court assigns a case number. You then need to serve the respondent (deliver copies of the filing). After service, the court schedules a hearing. We'll provide a checklist of next steps.
