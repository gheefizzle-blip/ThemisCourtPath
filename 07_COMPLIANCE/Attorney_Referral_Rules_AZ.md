## Key Finding: Arizona Permits Non-Lawyer Referral Fees

Arizona is one of the most permissive states in the US for attorney referral arrangements. Effective January 1, 2021, Arizona:

1. **Eliminated Rule 7.2(b)** — Previously prohibited attorneys from giving anything of value for referrals. Now permitted.
2. **Eliminated Rule 5.4** — Previously prohibited non-lawyer ownership of law firms. Now permitted under Alternative Business Structure (ABS) program.

---

## What This Means for Themis Court Path

### Permitted Activities
- Charging attorneys a flat fee per referral ($500 conversion fee model)
- Charging attorneys a percentage of revenue from referred clients
- Receiving payment from attorneys for client introductions
- Operating a for-profit attorney matching/referral service
- Potentially forming an ABS where Themis has economic interest in a law practice

### Requirements
- Referral fees must be **reasonable** (not unconscionable)
- Client must **consent in writing** to the referral fee arrangement
- At least one **Arizona-licensed attorney** must be involved in the representation
- Fee arrangement must be **disclosed to the client**

### What We CANNOT Do
- Practice law (give legal advice, represent clients, draft custom legal arguments)
- Guarantee specific outcomes
- Share fees without disclosure
- Operate in other states under Arizona rules (each state has its own rules)

---

## ABS Program (Alternative Business Structures)

Arizona's ABS program (Rule 31.1) allows entities that include non-lawyers to provide legal services, subject to:
- Registration with the Arizona Supreme Court
- Compliance with ethics rules
- At least one Arizona-licensed attorney involved
- Annual reporting

**Potential future path**: Themis could apply for ABS status to offer deeper legal service integration (Phase 4+).

## Comparison: Arizona vs. Other States

| State | Referral fees to non-lawyers? | Non-lawyer ownership? |
|-------|-------------------------------|----------------------|
| **Arizona** | **YES (since 2021)** | **YES (ABS program)** |
| California | NO (Rule 7.2) | NO |
| New York | NO (Rule 7.2) | NO |
| Texas | NO (Rule 7.03) | NO |
| Utah | LIMITED (regulatory sandbox) | LIMITED |
| Washington DC | YES (limited) | NO |

Arizona's position is a significant competitive advantage for Themis.
